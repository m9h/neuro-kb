## What the paper covers

Heavy-Tailed Self-Regularization predicts that well-trained layers converge to a power-law spectrum exponent α ≈ 2; Martin's 2026 RG paper sharpens this into a robustness prediction (checkpoints closer to α = 2 should drift less under realistic input perturbations). Using `wwj`:

1. **Cross-modality spectral survey** (Experiment 1) — five neuroimaging / fMRI FM checkpoints; mean |α - 2| ranges from 0.12 (brainage) to 4.73 (MindEye per-subject finetune).
2. **Paired cohort robustness sweep** (Experiment 2 + 3) — 20 WAND subjects, TorchIO perturbations; the α-closer model shows 3× lower CLS drift under bias field. Wilcoxon p = 0.00022, replicated in 9/10 perturbation seeds.
3. **Interventional ablation** (Experiment 4) — `wwj.alpha_loss(target=2.0, weight=0.01)` drives mean α to 2.00 within 1000 steps, yields uniformly higher noise-robustness across σ ∈ {0.05, ..., 1.6}.
4. **Vuong LRT validity check** — multiple over-fit checkpoint layers fail the power-law-vs-exponential test, a pitfall the reference WeightWatcher silently misses.
