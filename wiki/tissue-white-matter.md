# White Matter

White matter is brain tissue primarily composed of myelinated axons that connect different regions of gray matter. It appears white due to the high lipid content of myelin sheaths surrounding the axonal fibers. White matter exhibits highly anisotropic properties across multiple physical domains.

## Microstructure

White matter consists of:
- **Axons**: Nerve fiber projections with diameters ranging 0.2-20 μm
- **Myelin sheaths**: Lipid-rich wrapping around axons (20-50% by volume)
- **Oligodendrocytes**: Cells that produce myelin
- **Astrocytes**: Glial support cells
- **Extracellular space**: ~20% volume fraction

The parallel arrangement of myelinated fibers creates strong directional anisotropy in diffusion, electrical, acoustic, and mechanical properties.

## Diffusion Properties

White matter exhibits restricted and hindered diffusion due to cellular barriers:

| Parameter | Value | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Parallel diffusivity (D∥) | 1.7 × 10⁻⁹ m²/s | Along fiber direction |
| Perpendicular diffusivity (D⊥) | 0.2-0.6 × 10⁻⁹ m²/s | Across fibers |
| Fractional anisotropy (FA) | 0.4-0.8 | Measure of directional preference |
| Mean diffusivity (MD) | 0.7-0.9 × 10⁻⁹ m²/s | Average across directions |

### Biophysical Models

**NODDI (Neurite Orientation Dispersion and Density Imaging)**:
- Intra-axonal compartment: C1Stick with fixed D∥ = 1.7 × 10⁻⁹ m²/s
- Extra-axonal compartment: Zeppelin with tortuosity constraint
- CSF compartment: Ball with D = 3.0 × 10⁻⁹ m²/s
- Watson distribution for fiber orientation dispersion

**SANDI (Soma and Neurite Density Imaging)**:
- Separates soma and neurite contributions
- Accounts for cell body effects in gray-white matter boundaries

## Electrical Properties

White matter shows frequency-dependent electrical anisotropy:

| Frequency | σ∥ (S/m) | σ⊥ (S/m) | εᵣ∥ | εᵣ⊥ |
|-----------|----------|----------|------|------|
| 128 Hz | 0.126 | 0.083 | - | - |
| 1 kHz | 0.142 | 0.094 | 150 | 120 |
| 1 MHz | 0.380 | 0.246 | 45 | 35 |

The anisotropy ratio (σ∥/σ⊥) is typically 1.5-2.0, reflecting the preferential conduction along myelinated fibers.

## Acoustic Properties

For transcranial focused ultrasound applications:

| Property | Value | Frequency |
|----------|-------|-----------|
| Speed of sound | 1552-1580 m/s | 0.5-4 MHz |
| Acoustic impedance | 1.58 MRayl | - |
| Attenuation coefficient | 0.6 dB/cm/MHz | 0.5-4 MHz |
| Density | 1041 kg/m³ | - |

White matter has intermediate acoustic properties between gray matter and CSF, contributing to skull-corrected focusing in TUS simulations.

## Mechanical Properties

From Kuhl CANN studies of brain tissue mechanics:

| Property | Value | Notes |
|----------|-------|-------|
| Shear modulus | 2.8-4.2 kPa | Anisotropic, stiffer along fibers |
| Poisson's ratio | 0.45-0.49 | Nearly incompressible |
| Viscoelastic time constant | ~0.1 s | Frequency-dependent |

White matter is mechanically stiffer than gray matter due to myelin content, with ~3.4× greater displacement under ultrasound radiation force compared to corpus callosum.

## Modeling in SpinDoctor.jl and sbi4dwi

**SpinDoctor.jl**: Implements finite element solutions for diffusion MRI simulation in white matter bundles and fiber geometries. Supports:
- Matrix formalism with Laplace eigenfunctions
- BTPDE solving for complex gradient sequences  
- Multi-compartment models with permeable membranes

**sbi4dwi**: JAX-accelerated simulation-based inference for white matter microstructure:
- Ball+Stick, Ball+Zeppelin composite models
- NODDI and SANDI implementations
- Neural posterior estimation for parameter recovery
- Multi-fidelity training with analytical and FEM simulators

**MCMRSimulator.jl**: Monte Carlo random walk simulations in realistic white matter substrates generated by the CONFIG algorithm, providing ground truth for validating analytical models.

## Pathological Changes

White matter pathology affects multiple physical properties:
- **Demyelination**: Reduced anisotropy, increased perpendicular diffusivity
- **Axonal loss**: Decreased parallel diffusivity, reduced FA
- **Edema**: Increased MD, altered T2 relaxation
- **Inflammation**: Changed electrical conductivity

## Clinical Applications

- **Diffusion tensor imaging (DTI)**: Mapping white matter tracts
- **Tractography**: Reconstruction of fiber pathways
- **Deep brain stimulation**: Electrical field modeling requires anisotropic conductivity
- **Focused ultrasound**: Skull correction and targeting through white matter
- **MR-ARFI**: Displacement encoding for tissue characterization
